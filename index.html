Why Study MBBS in Nepal

  • Affordable Tuition Fees: Studying MBBS in Nepal is relatively more affordable compared to many Western countries, making it a cost-effective option for medical education.

  • High-Quality Education: Nepalese medical universities offer a high standard of education with a curriculum that aligns with international standards, often recognized by global medical councils.

  • English Medium Instruction: Most medical universities in Nepal teach in English, which is advantageous for international students and eases the learning process.

  • Modern Infrastructure: Nepal's medical colleges are equipped with modern infrastructure and facilities, including advanced laboratories and well-maintained hospitals for clinical practice

  • Cultural Proximity: For students from South Asia, Nepal offers cultural and geographical proximity, which can make the transition smoother and more comfortable.

  • No Entrance Exams for Some Universities: Some medical universities in Nepal do not require international students to pass stringent entrance exams, simplifying the admission process.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Are theWhat is the duration of the MBBS program in Nepal?

+

The MBBS program in Nepal typically lasts for 5.5 years, including a one-year internship​ (Careers360 Study Abroad)​​ (V4Edu)​.

Q: What are the eligibility criteria for Indian students to study MBBS in Nepal?

+

Indian students must have completed 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and English, securing at least 50% in each subject. They must also qualify for the NEET exam​ (V4Edu)​​ (Careers360 Study Abroad)​.

Q: Is NEET mandatory for admission to MBBS in Nepal?

+

Yes, NEET is mandatory for Indian students to secure admission and practice in India after completing their MBBS in Nepal​ (Careers360 Study Abroad)​​ (Careers360 Study Abroad)​

Q: What is the fee structure for MBBS in Nepal?

+

The fees range from INR 40 to 60 lakhs for the entire course duration, depending on the university. Public universities tend to have lower fees compared to private institutions​ (Careers360 Study Abroad)​.

Q: What is the medium of instruction for MBBS in Nepal?

+

The medium of instruction is English, which is advantageous for international students​ (Careers360 Study Abroad)​​ (V4Edu)​.

Q: Are degrees from Nepal medical colleges recognized internationally?

+

Yes, degrees from medical colleges in Nepal are recognized by global medical bodies like WFME and FAIMER, and are also recognized by the Medical Council of India (MCI)​ (Careers360 Study Abroad)​​ (V4Edu).

Q: What is the admission process for MBBS in Nepal?

+

The process involves: Applying through the university’s official website. Submitting required documents. Qualifying NEET. Attending counseling sessions if applicable​ (V4Edu)​.

Q: What are the living costs for students in Nepal?

+

Living expenses, including accommodation, food, healthcare, and other necessities, generally range between INR 10,000 to 15,000 per month depending on lifestyle and location​ (Careers360 Study Abroad)​.

Q: Can students work while studying MBBS in Nepal?

+

Students are generally advised to focus on their studies due to the demanding nature of the MBBS program. However, some universities might allow part-time work under specific conditions​ (V4Edu)​​ (Select Your University)​.
